## Signature frameworks / POV

1. **Correlation studies, not opinions.** Cyrus's signature move is to publish a data-driven analysis of what *actually* correlates with Google rankings or AI citations. The "5 Data-Backed Features" study is the template; he's run similar studies for years.
2. **"The Long Tail Is Back, and It's Brands"** — his recurring thesis: in 2026, Google rewards brands with strong entity authority, not just well-optimized pages. See https://sparktoro.com/blog/5-strategic-features-that-predict-survival-in-the-zero-click-era/
3. **"Clicks may not matter" (the controversial position)** — In May 2026 he published / was featured in a Near Media episode titled "Google Lied About Click Data — Here's What Actually Drives Rankings in 2026." Cyrus's take: click-through rate is a weaker ranking signal than Google admits. Other factors dominate.
4. **23-factor analysis for AI citations** — past research where he analyzed 23 on-page / off-page factors and ranked them by correlation with AI Overview citation.
5. **"Test, don't guess"** — methodological core. His consulting is built on running controlled tests rather than applying industry folklore.

## Useful angles for repurposing

- **Run your own correlation study** — Cyrus's template is reproducible. Pick a sample of N sites, code them on a feature list, publish correlation coefficients on a Substack, push on LinkedIn. You don't need N=400; N=50 sites in your vertical is enough to start.
- **"Brands > pages" thesis** — pair with Lily Ray's E-E-A-T and Kevin Indig's pipeline-channel framing. Three independent voices, one story.
- **The 23 AI-citation factors** — older but still the most cited source. Great "definitive guide" material.
- **Substack → LinkedIn → press cycle** — Cyrus's distribution pattern is a clean case study. (1) Publish on Substack. (2) Post on LinkedIn with a hook. (3) Get picked up by Lily Ray / Rand / Kevin. (4) Cover at PPC Land, etc. Reproducible.
- **"Test, don't guess"** — methodology-first brand. Borrow the framing for any "we actually measured it" post.
